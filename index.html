MA.PH.EAN

Meet our multifunctional delivery robot

The Creators

Maphean was created to follow a black trail until it reaches the end, then return to its starting point. It can pick up objects with its claw and drop them off at a destination. With both functions combined, Maphean aims to become a future delivery robot.

Purpose of Maphean

Maphean is designed to autonomously follow paths and transport items. It's a simple yet powerful step toward robots that can assist in everyday logistics or automation tasks.

Blueprint - For following a black trail

Explanation: Black Line Pickup Routine

Sensor Initialization: The robot continuously reads values from its left, center, and right line-following sensors to detect its position relative to the black line.

Line Following: Based on the sensor values, the robot adjusts its movement to stay aligned with the black track. It goes straight if the center sensor sees black, or turns if the left or right sensors go off track.

Object Handling: If none of the sensors detect black (robot is on the object zone), it triggers a full pickup and drop sequence. The claw opens, moves forward, grabs the object, turns, and deposits it before continuing.

Explanation: Object Pickup Code

Sensor Initialization: Variables for the left, center, and right sensors are initialized. These will store real-time values used to control movement.

Sensor Reading Loop: In a continuous loop, the robot gathers updated readings from its three line-following sensors to determine where it is relative to the line.

Move Forward: If the center sensor sees a light surface (value > 2700), it means the robot has left the black line and needs to keep moving forward to re-find it.

Close Claw: If the center sensor detects a dark spot (value < 2450), the robot stops and activates the claw motor to grab an object.

180 Turn: The robot spins in place using opposite motor directions to make a full 180-degree turn, preparing to head back with the object.

Move Back & Release: It reverses briefly while opening the claw slightly to release the object at its target destination.

Left Drift Correction: If the left sensor detects white, the robot has drifted right and must turn left to return to the line.

Right Drift Correction: If the right sensor sees white, the robot has veered left and needs to turn right to correct its path.
